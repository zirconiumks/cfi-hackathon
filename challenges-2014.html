Prepare for Emergency Response

Challenge Partner: World Bank

Problem Statement

Prepare for natural disasters or other emergencies that occur in Indian cities (e.g. Uttarakhand) where there is a high traffic of visitors as tourists or pilgrims.

Proposed Solution

Build an application for the visitors to self-report their location and travel details at regular intervals. Build an admin console to track these visitors and take rescue measures in case of emergency. High-level ideas for your solution: a)Store & process high volume of visitor information. b) Receive “check-in” data from multiple devices/input mechanisms. c)Visualize visitor information on an interactive real-time map from the admin console.

Web, Smart Phones, Regular Phones

Frontend Development, Backend Development, Mobile Developer, Hardware Hacker, Data Visualization

Follow the Money

Challenge Partner: World Bank

Problem Statement

Money from World Bank supported projects impacts citizens and businesses directly through the contracts that are awarded for public goods or services - such as for laying roads or building schools. Surprisingly, not many many know about these contracts we’re investing in, even when they represent large amounts of public money meant for their neighbourhood.

Citizen oversight of who gets this money to do what and where can deter corruption and improve quality of delivery.

Knowledge of upcoming tender notices can create a level playing field for private sector, and be a useful resource for business owners.

Proposed Solution

Build an application for citizens to locate if a contract awarded out of World Bank funded project is for their neighbourhood, what is supposed to be delivered, and who got the contract. Enable citizens to share their feedback, pictures, and other files to help track progress and provide crowd oversight. Add features for business owners to get notifications or alerts of tender notices coming up in their industry or area.

High-level ideas for your solution:

  • Geo code and map open contracting data
  • Make data searchable by industry or topic
  • Receive “feedback” from multiple devices/input mechanisms
  • Create notifications for when new a tender/contract appears in the data

Expected to run on

Smart Phones and Web

Skills that might be useful

Frontend Development, Backend Development, Maps (API & Tools), Data Mining & Visualization

Daily Food Requirement Simulator

NGO Partner: Akshaya Patra

Problem Statement

Akshaya Patra currently serves more than 1.2 million meals daily through their network in 9 states in India. To ensure operational efficiency, each Kitchen should prepare meals in exact quantities everyday to prevent either shortage or wastage. This is currently done by collecting the approximate attendance estimates from teachers by the delivery staff. They also take feedback on the previous day’s quantities. However, the entire process is manual, time consuming and tedious. Teachers have no access to any mechanism to know how much food is required for consumption.

Proposed Solution

To create an application that can predict the quantity of food required in each operational region based on the historical data. The application should make it easy for delivery teams to collect metrics and share them with the Kitchens. The Kitchens should be able to see prediction numbers for quantities of each part of the meal (e.g. 125 servings of Rice, 100 Servings of Dal etc). The application should be able to take attendance numbers on a daily basis and adjust the predictions for the next day.

Expected to run on

Web and/or Mobile

Skills that might be useful

Frontend, Backend, Data Visualization, Designer, Prediction APIs

Voter Analytics

NGO Partner: Bangalore Political Action Committee

Problem Statement

One important and underserved need is to analyze post-poll data, especially in regions where voter turnout is lower than estimated. Several discrepancies contribute to low voter turnout - from mistakes in the electoral rolls, to mismatched voter IDs and missing names on the polling days. It is not a trivial task to decipher the reasons why the turnout was low, and analyse how this can be avoided in the next elections. So there is a sincere need for a reliable voter analytics system for post-poll analysis.

Proposed Solution

Design a voter analytics framework and working prototype to understand the voter trends in Bangalore.

  • Including a way to aggregate the voter turnout in specific constituencies.
  • Visualize voter turnout on maps (choropleth map - http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Choropleth_map)
  • Use alliance data where available
  • Show where independents have caused significant impact in swings
  • Show where low voter turnout has benefitted a certain candidate
  • Show how turnout has been affected by delimitation changes
  • If no 2014 data is available, run analytics on the state elections in 2013

Expected to run on

Web

Skills that might be useful

Frontend Development, Backend Development, UX Design

Let the Voters Data Speak

Challenge Partners: Volunteers for Better India, Bangalore Political Action Committee

Problem Statement

Though electoral rolls without photographs are published as PDF files on the websites of various Chief Electoral officers (CEO), less than 10% of them are in English as per Election Commission’s (ECI) directives. The rest are published in local languages. We have extracted text from the English PDF files, analysed the quality and suggested solutions to various quality issues in the rolls. This helps ECI to focus their limited resources on reviewing a few thousand problematic rolls vs. trying to find anomalies and do verifications based on 800 mln voters.

Proposed Solution

Develop a set of applications that can 1) Extract Indic text in Unicode from the PDF electoral rolls. 2) After extracting the Indic text, transliterate the contents to English.

Web, Desktop

SITA - School Infrastructure Tracking Application

Challenge Partner: American India Foundation

Problem Statement

The Right to Education (RtE) Act 2009 mandates certain infrastructure norms for each school. Tracking the implementation of building school infrastructure as per norms, their maintenance, and feedback from various stakeholders to the Government authorities, and timely action, all on a large scale is a challenge, which American India Foundation is trying to resolve.

Proposed Solution

A mobile-based application that will allow stakeholders (SMC members, NGO staff, Government officials) to track the status of infrastructure facilities in schools, like toilets, drinking water, barrier-free access etc., and provide feedback on their maintenance. This GPS enabled application will allow users to upload the photographs of the ground reality, and enter the status of the infrastructure from a list of available parameters. This information will automatically be uploaded to a website that will show updates for each school on a map, and generate heat-maps showing the status, and improvement, of school infrastructure across a state.

Cycle Rickshaw on Call

Challenge Partner: American India Foundation

Problem Statement

A significant portion of cycle rickshaw pullers’ working hours are spent searching or waiting for passengers. On the other hand, passengers are also seen searching for rickshaws. The demand-supply mismatch differs across locations, with concentration of cycle rickshaws in high traffic zones, and passengers in interior lanes having to walk.

Proposed Solution

A mobile application that can be downloaded on low-cost phones for a “rickshaw-on-call service”, converting the idle time of rickshaw pullers to productive time and making cycle rickshaws available at the door step. You could refer to other apps in the market like Uber, but build it to minimize device interaction and time spent on the app as most of these rickshaw-pullers aren’t educated.

Digital Green

Challenge Partner: NASSCOM Foundation

Problem Statement

At Digital Green, we train frontline workers in rural villages to asynchronously produce and screen videos to fellow farmers on techniques that can boost farm productivity. We are looking to build an interactive voice response (IVR) system to crowdsource feedback on the videos farmers like and those that they adopt.

Proposed Solution

We propose to build an IVR-based polling system to survey farmers on whether they are interested or adopted the practices that they watched on video during the previous two months. This system would take our existing farmer data (including mobile numbers) and make automated outbound calls to farmers. Farmers could press touchtone numbers (e.g., “1” for interested, “2” for not interested”). If a farmer selects the “interested” option, the farmer would then be asked whether they adopted the practice on their farm. A REST API would be developed to link the IVR survey data with our existing farmer database.

Web and/or Mobile

Skills that might be useful

Mobile Development, Backend Development, API Development

Mobile App for TeachAIDS

Challenge Partner: TeachAIDS

Problem Statement

Despite millions spent on AIDS awareness campaigns, a significant lack of knowledge about HIV and AIDS persists. The problem with traditional approaches is that the association with taboo topics and high stigma has led to many curricula not being taught, not being effective, or even being banned by law. This is particularly true in India, with the third largest HIV-positive population globally.

TeachAIDS has created breakthrough software that decouples HIV education from sex education using a culturally appropriate approach, which has had tremendous success in India. TeachAIDS would like to bring this content from web to mobile to expand its reach.

Proposed Solution

Build a simple, easy-to-use Android or iOS app that can supplement the TeachAIDS interactive software on the web and reach mobile users for expanded reach.

MVP: For this challenge, using existing formats of the TeachAIDS software available for free download on their website (teachaids.org/software), we would like your team to make either an Android or iOS application that is true to the original computer-based version. The solution should minimize file size while maximizing video quality.

Rescue Me

Challenge Partner: Rescue Me

Problem Statement

Victims have a very short time to react when they are in the situations of distress. Usually they resort to calling out for help with display of physical resistance. Such a call for help may or may not be heard depending on the location and surroundings of the incident. Most of the time, the trusted family and friends, are far away from the location where the call for help can be heard. This results in situations where rescue of the victim becomes very difficulty and maintaining their safety is highly unlikely.

Proposed Solution

The aim is to create an application that can be triggered with the minimum number of steps to initiate a distress call. The distress call sends our alerts to a selected group of family, friends and under certain conditions also to local emergency services. The app should be smart to reach out for help to people who are most likely to be nearby. It should be extensible to also record location data since the time the distress call was initiated and closed.

Expected to run on

Web and/or Mobile

Skills that might be useful

Mobile App Development, App Design, Maps API, Offline Maps

Zariya

Challenge Partner: Zariya

Mission Statement

Zariya empowers survivors of gender-based violence (domestic and sexual) by providing them with a safe and reliable medium to access Actionable information, Service providers, Enforcement. By accelerating the knowledge and action flows that are critical prerequisites to legal action and the pursuit of justice, Zariya makes women the authors of their own process. The platform fights under-reporting and lack of access to actionable legal information by creating an online space of transparency and accountability.

Proposed Solution

Create a native mobile app (iOS or Android) which replicates the behavior of http://54.186.110.31/ on a mobile device - including a Multi Step Form to report an incident: http://54.186.110.31/submitReport (The form allows survivors to file send Zariya an anonymous report about the sexual assault incident with the location and time stamp data); and View submitted reports on a map: http://54.186.110.31/viewMap. Zariya’s focus is on collecting data about incidents women in India face, and make the frequency and location of incidents available while keeping the survivors’ identity anonymous.

Native Mobile
